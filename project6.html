Introduction

This project focused on analyzing FIFA World Cup data using SQL to uncover patterns in match outcomes, team performance, player stats, and historical records. Using data from multiple World Cup tournaments and team records, SQL queries were executed to answer strategic and analytical questions about the event’s history.

Objective

To explore FIFA World Cup history and match records through SQL queries — extracting trends in goals, wins, player performances, and match outcomes while identifying notable records and team rankings.

Methodology

Database Exploration:

  1. Reviewed schema and relationships between match, squad, and tournament tables.
  2. Identified foreign keys (teams, match_id, player info) for joins.

Historical Tournament Analysis:

  • Total matches played in World Cup history.
  • List of World Cup winners by year.
  • Number of matches per tournament year.
  • Total and average goals scored in each World Cup.

    • Team & Player Performance Analysis:

  • Most successful teams based on titles won.
  • Teams with the most finals appearances.
  • Top 5 goal-scoring players in the 2022 World Cup.
  • Countries that hosted the most World Cup matches.

    • Ranking & Match Records:

    • Ranked teams based on FIFA rankings in the 2022 tournament.
    • Identified top 5 goal-scoring players from the 2022 squad.
    • Counted final appearances for each team.

    Results

    Delivered a comprehensive set of insights including:

    • Total matches, wins, and goals per tournament.
    • Historical records of World Cup champions and finals appearances.
    • Top-performing teams and players by goals and titles.
    • Insights into host countries and match distribution.
    • Generated rankings and player leaderboards for the 2022 World Cup.

    Enabled a data-driven view of team dominance, goal patterns, and match outcomes over World Cup history.
